Privacy Policy

Effective · 23 May 2026

Grace Journal (“we,” “our,” or “us”) is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our mobile application and related services (“Services”).

only what we need ✦

  • Device information (model, operating system, app version)

  • Approximate location data (if location-based features are enabled)

4Storage & Security

your stuff stays yours ✦

4.1 Where your notes live

Your notes and journal entries are stored as a plaintext copy locally on your device (so the app works offline and stays fast) and, when you sign in, are also stored in encrypted form in your account on our servers. Thankfulness entries, prayers, prayer lists, and prayer requests are synced to your account so they are available across your devices.

4.2 Encryption & access

Your notes are encrypted on your device before they are sent to our servers. The encryption key is generated on your device and stored in your device’s Keychain, and is synced between your own devices through your personal iCloud Keychain. This key is never sent to our servers.

Because we never receive the key, our servers and our hosting providers only ever hold encrypted versions of your notes and cannot read them. Data is also protected in transit (when it moves between your device and our servers) and stored encrypted at rest.

4.3 Important: recovering your notes

Because the key that unlocks your notes lives in your device Keychain and your personal iCloud Keychain — and never on our servers — your access to your encrypted notes depends on your access to that key.

If you lose access to your iCloud account or iCloud Keychain (for example, you are locked out of your Apple Account, you disable iCloud Keychain, or you delete and reinstall the app on a device that can no longer reach your iCloud Keychain), the key may no longer be available. In that case your encrypted notes cannot be recovered — not by you, and not by us, because we never hold the key needed to unlock them.

To protect against this, we strongly recommend keeping a separate copy of any entries that matter to you (see 4.7), and keeping your Apple Account and iCloud Keychain access secure.

keep a backup ✦

4.4 Backups & retention

Encrypted backups that may contain note data are retained for a limited period (up to 30 days) after deletion for disaster recovery and fraud prevention. During this retention window, deleted content is not available to other users and cannot be accessed in readable form, and is only used for disaster recovery purposes.

After the retention window, we make commercially reasonable efforts to purge deleted data from backups and provider systems.

4.5 Device responsibility

Because the app keeps a plaintext copy on your device for offline editing, protecting that device is your responsibility. We are not responsible for disclosure, modification, or deletion of note content that results from device compromise (for example jailbreaking, rooting, installing untrusted software, or granting other apps access to your device storage).

We strongly recommend using a device passcode, enabling biometric locks, and keeping your OS and apps up to date.

stay safe ✦

4.6 When we may be required to disclose

We will not disclose your notes to third parties except: (a) as required by law (for example a lawful court order); (b) to comply with a valid legal process; or (c) to protect rights or safety where legally permitted. Because we only hold encrypted versions of your notes and not the key, any notes we disclose from server storage would be in encrypted form. We will disclose only what is required and will notify you unless legally prohibited.

4.7 Exporting your data

The best way to keep a readable copy of your notes is from within the app on your device, where they can be read and exported in plaintext. We encourage you to do this for any entries that matter to you.

You may also request a copy of the personal information we hold about you. For account and profile information we will provide this in a readable form. For your notes, however, the only copy our servers hold is encrypted, and we do not have the key — so any notes provided from our servers will be in encrypted form, which is only usable on a device that holds your key. Deleted notes are not included in exports, even if they exist in backups.

4.8 How to remove your data

You can delete notes or close your account in the app. After deletion we remove data from active systems promptly; encrypted backups that may contain deleted data will be retained for the 30-day backup window described above and then purged according to our provider practices.

To request your data or removal, email us at contact@gracejournal.co. We aim to respond within 20 working days.
